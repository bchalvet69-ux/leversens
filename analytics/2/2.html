What is Monte Carlo?

Monte Carlo simulation generates 1 000 possible future price paths by randomly sampling from historical returns using a Geometric Brownian Motion (GBM) model. Each path uses the asset's real drift (average return) and volatility to simulate 30 days forward. The result is a probability distribution of where the price might end up — not a single prediction, but a range of scenarios.

How to Read the Chart

The white median line shows the most likely path. The shaded bands represent confidence intervals: 50% of simulations fall within the darker band, 90% within the lighter one. The red P5 and green P95 dotted lines mark extreme scenarios. If 95% of simulations end between $X and $Y, you have a practical range for setting take-profit and stop-loss levels. Wider bands mean higher uncertainty and risk.
