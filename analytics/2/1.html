What is GARCH?

GARCH (Generalized AutoRegressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity) models how volatility evolves over time. The key insight is volatility clustering: when markets are turbulent, they tend to stay turbulent. GARCH(1,1) estimates tomorrow's volatility based on today's return shock and recent volatility levels. The dotted yellow line shows the 30-day forecast — where volatility is expected to go based on current market conditions.

How to Use This

GARCH is essential for position sizing: when predicted volatility spikes, reduce exposure; when it's low, you can increase it. Compare assets — BTC typically shows ~50% annualized volatility vs SPY's ~15%, which directly impacts portfolio allocation weights. The forecast helps anticipate whether volatility is mean-reverting (calming down) or persisting (staying elevated), guiding short-term risk management decisions.
