Pair Trading Strategy

Pair trading is a market-neutral strategy that profits from the relative movement of two correlated assets. The idea is simple: GOLD and NEM (both gold miners) tend to move together over time. When one temporarily diverges from the other, we bet they'll converge back. We first run an OLS regression to quantify the normal relationship, then compute a Z-score measuring how far the spread has drifted from its mean. When the Z-score exceeds ±2 standard deviations, we take a position expecting mean reversion. The strategy is "market-neutral" because we're simultaneously long one asset and short the other — overall market direction doesn't matter, only the relative movement counts.

Trading Rules

Buy when Z-score < -2 (NEM is cheap relative to GOLD — go long NEM, short GOLD). Sell when Z-score > +2 (NEM is expensive — go short NEM, long GOLD). Close at Z = 0 (the pair has returned to equilibrium, take profit). The P&L chart shows the cumulative backtest performance of these rules applied over the historical period.
