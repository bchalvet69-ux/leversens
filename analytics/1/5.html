Bollinger Bands Strategy

Bollinger Bands consist of three lines: a 20-period SMA (simple moving average) in the center, and two bands plotted at ±2 standard deviations above and below. The bands automatically widen during volatile periods and contract during calm periods. The key concept is the squeeze: when the bands narrow, volatility is compressed and a breakout is imminent — you trade the direction of the breakout. When price touches the upper band, the asset is stretched to the upside and may mean-revert back to the SMA. When price hits the lower band, it's stretched to the downside and a bounce toward the SMA is likely. The most reliable signals combine a band touch with confirmation from other indicators (RSI oversold + lower band = strong buy signal).
