ICT Breaker Blocks

An Order Block is the last candle before a strong directional impulse — it marks where institutional orders were placed. When the opposite side of an Order Block gets broken by a later close, it becomes a Breaker Block, reversing its role from support to resistance (or vice versa). These zones are high-probability retest areas for entries.

OHLC Statistical Mapping

Each day's session Open is used as an anchor. From the last 20 daily candles we compute the average Distribution (move in the candle's direction) and Manipulation (wick against the trend). These levels project around each session's Open: ±Distribution (expansion targets) and ±Manipulation (fake-out / stop-hunt levels). You can see 3 sessions, each with its own set of levels.
